01 · About

Building the infrastructure behind the apps

I'm a Computer Science Engineering student who got tired of watching applications break the moment real traffic hit them — so I went looking for the layer underneath: compute, storage, networking, and the automation that holds it all together. That search led me to AWS, and I haven't stopped digging since.

Most of what I know didn't come from a syllabus. It came from spinning up EC2 instances at 1 a.m., wiring a Boto3 script to a bucket that refused to cooperate, and reading IAM error messages until they finally made sense. I care about Cloud Engineering because it rewards that kind of stubbornness — every fixed permission policy or automated teardown script is a small, provable win.

On the backend side, I build with Python and FastAPI, treating APIs as contracts that should be predictable, documented, and boring in the best way. I'm currently deepening my grip on Lambda, event-driven design, and infrastructure as code — because the goal isn't just to make the cloud work, it's to make it repeatable.
